# Dimensionality Selection Study

**Date:** 2026-02-16
**Attribution:** TEST-BRIDGE-MVP1-DIMENSION-SELECTION-001

## Research Question

Does information geometry select a preferred spatial dimension d? Specifically, do Ising observers on d-dimensional lattices show non-trivial dependence on d, particularly whether d=3 or d=4 is special?

## Method

1. Generate d-dimensional lattice graphs for d=1,2,3,4
2. Compute exact Ising Fisher matrices
3. Measure: near-diagonal ratio, spectral gap, effective rank, beta_c, girth
4. Look for non-trivial d-dependence

## Configurations

- d=1: 1D chain, L=9 (N=9 vertices, m=8 edges)
- d=2: 2D square lattice, L=3 (N=9 vertices, m=12 edges)
- d=3: 3D cubic lattice, L=2 (N=8 vertices, m=12 edges)
- d=4: 4D hypercubic lattice, L=2 (N=16 vertices, m=32 edges)
- d=5: Skipped (2^32 states infeasible)

**Total configurations tested:** 16

## Limitations

- Small system sizes (N ≤ 16 vertices) due to exact enumeration constraint
- Different linear sizes L for different dimensions (L=9 for d=1, L=2 for d=4)
- Finite-size effects may obscure genuine d-dependence
- d=5 not tested (too many states for exact enumeration)
