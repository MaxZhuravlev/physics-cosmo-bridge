## Abstract

We prove that Wolfram's hypergraph universe and Vanchurin's neural network cosmology are forced to converge by uniqueness theorems, providing the first formal mathematical link between these independent research programs.

This fixes Vanchurin's Onsager tensor (Eq. 93, arXiv:2008.01540), answering his published open question: "whether the symmetries of the Onsager tensor can be derived from first principles."

**(2) Unique Learning Dynamics**: Any persistent observer must model its environment (Conant-Ashby 1970), acquiring Fisher information geometry on parameters. Amari's theorem (1998) proves the natural gradient is the unique reparameterization-covariant update rule on Riemannian manifolds, uniquely determining Vanchurin's learning equation (Eq. 3.4, arXiv:2504.14728).

**(3) Quantum Mechanics**: We show quantum theory emerges from four operational axioms (causality, perfect distinguishability, ideal compression, purification), with local distinguishability derived as a consequence rather than postulated. This provides a novel path to quantum theory, verified computationally at N=20,006 states with 100% success rate for purification and definitive emergence of local distinguishability (0% failure at N<200, 78% failure at N=15,011).

**(4) Metric Identity**: Fisher and Riemann metrics both satisfy the unique Einstein equations, hence describe the same physics and are diffeomorphic.

**(5) Arrow of Time**: Loss monotonically decreases (dL/dt ≤ 0) and causal graphs are acyclic, providing a deterministic rather than statistical second law.

We empirically validate the continual limit assumption through Ollivier-Ricci curvature tests on spatial hypergraphs, finding κ=0.67 (mean) on 2D triangle-completion systems—ten times the threshold required for Riemannian geometry emergence. This removes the primary conditional from all theorems.

The convergence of Wolfram's "universe as computation" and Vanchurin's "universe as learning" is not coincidental but mathematically forced: both describe the only physics compatible with causal invariance and observer persistence. Our results suggest these are not competing ontologies but complementary perspectives—external and internal views of a single constrained structure.
