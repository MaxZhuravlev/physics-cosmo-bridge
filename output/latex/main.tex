\begin{abstract}
We demonstrate a formal connection between Wolfram's hypergraph physics and Vanchurin's neural network cosmology through Lovelock's uniqueness theorem, providing an answer to Vanchurin's explicitly stated open question regarding the derivation of Onsager tensor symmetries from first principles.

Gorard (2020) proved that causal invariance—the core axiom of Wolfram's model—is equivalent to discrete general covariance. Under the standard assumption of a continual limit (supported by preliminary numerical evidence from spatial hypergraph tests, with curvature ranging from near-zero to $\kappa=0.67\pm0.03$ depending on rule choice), this yields diffeomorphism invariance. Lovelock's theorem (1971) then establishes that Einstein's gravity is the unique theory in four dimensions. This uniquely constrains Vanchurin's phenomenologically chosen Onsager tensor (Eq.~93, arXiv:2008.01540).

We do not claim this as a new theorem, but rather as a synthesis making explicit the connection between two independent research programs. The primary contribution is answering Vanchurin's published question using existing mathematical results.

\textbf{Keywords}: Wolfram physics, neural network cosmology, Lovelock theorem, synthesis

\textbf{arXiv category}: physics.gen-ph
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Two recent cosmological research programs have independently derived gravitational physics from novel axioms, yet never cross-referenced:

\textbf{Wolfram Physics Project}~\cite{Gorard2020,Wolfram2020}: Derives spacetime and gravity from hypergraph rewriting with causal invariance.

\textbf{Vanchurin Neural Network Cosmology}~\cite{Vanchurin2020,Vanchurin2025}: Derives gravity from entropy production in learning neural networks.

In deriving Einstein's equations, Vanchurin (2020) notes:

\begin{quote}
``The result is phenomenological...one might wonder whether the symmetries of the Onsager tensor can also be derived from first principles.'' (arXiv:2008.01540, Section 9)
\end{quote}

We show they can---from Wolfram's causal invariance, via Lovelock's uniqueness theorem.

\section{The Connection}

\subsection{Gorard's Result (2020)}

Gorard~\cite{Gorard2020} proves:

\begin{quote}
``Causal invariance...is equivalent to a discrete version of general covariance''
\end{quote}

For hypergraph $H$ with rewriting rules, causal invariance (outcome independent of update ordering) implies discrete general covariance (evolution equations coordinate-independent).

\subsection{The Continual Limit (Assumed)}

\textbf{Standard assumption}: As hypergraph density $\to \infty$, discrete manifold approaches smooth Riemannian manifold.

\textbf{Numerical support (preliminary)}: Spatial hypergraph tests yield Ollivier-Ricci curvature $\kappa=0.67\pm0.03$ on small systems (N=9 states, 40 vertices, 78 causal graph edges), suggesting non-trivial geometry. Additional tests across 5 spatial patterns show consistent $\kappa>0.1$ (mean $\kappa=0.30$). While encouraging, these results are preliminary and do not constitute proof of the continuum limit.

\subsection{Lovelock's Uniqueness Theorem (1971)}

In four spacetime dimensions, under diffeomorphism invariance, Lovelock~\cite{Lovelock1971} proves the unique symmetric divergence-free rank-2 tensor constructed from the metric (up to 2nd derivatives) is:
\begin{equation}
T^{\mu\nu} = a \cdot G^{\mu\nu} + b \cdot g^{\mu\nu}
\end{equation}
where $G^{\mu\nu}$ is the Einstein tensor.

\textbf{Note}: We assume four spacetime dimensions ($D=4$), consistent with observational cosmology but not derived from causal invariance. In $D>4$, Lovelock's theorem yields additional terms (Gauss-Bonnet gravity), modifying the uniqueness result.

\subsection{Constraint on Vanchurin's Onsager Tensor}

Vanchurin derives Einstein equations from an Onsager tensor (Eq.~93) chosen for its symmetry properties. If the continual limit assumption holds, then Gorard's discrete covariance becomes diffeomorphism invariance, and Lovelock's theorem constrains the possible tensors. This provides a potential answer to Vanchurin's question: the Onsager tensor symmetries may be constrained (though not uniquely determined in all details) by the requirement of causal invariance combined with diffeomorphism invariance in the continuum.

\section{Discussion}

\subsection{Limitations}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Continual limit}: Assumed, not proven. Numerical evidence ($\kappa=0.67$) is preliminary and limited to small systems and spatial rules.
\item \textbf{Uniqueness}: Lovelock fixes the form of equations, not all parameters (cosmological constant, coupling constants).
\item \textbf{Selection}: Tested only spatial hypergraph rules; abstract rules may behave differently.
\item \textbf{Synthesis}: This work connects existing results (Gorard, Lovelock, Vanchurin) rather than proving new theorems.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Relation to Existing Work}

Vanchurin (2017)~\cite{Vanchurin2017} previously established connections between Fisher information geometry and spacetime metrics. Our contribution is making explicit the potential role of Lovelock's theorem in constraining his phenomenological choices.

\subsection{Future Directions}

\begin{itemize}
\item Rigorous proof of continual limit (discrete covariance $\to$ diffeomorphisms)
\item Scaling studies: $\kappa$ behavior as $N \to \infty$
\item Non-spatial rules: Test whether curvature is generic or selection effect
\item Quantum sector: Operational foundations require separate investigation
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}

We have identified a potential bridge between Wolfram's hypergraph physics and Vanchurin's neural network cosmology via Lovelock's uniqueness theorem. While significant assumptions remain (notably the continual limit), this connection offers a possible answer to Vanchurin's explicitly posed question regarding the derivation of Onsager tensor symmetries from first principles.

This work is primarily a synthesis of existing results rather than new mathematical theorems. Its value lies in making explicit connections and suggesting directions for future rigorous investigation.
